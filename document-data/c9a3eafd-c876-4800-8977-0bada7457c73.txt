PIXELOOP LLC Founders Equity & Contribution Agreement This Founders Equity & Contribution Agreement (the “Agreement”) is entered into and effective as of ______________, 2026 (the “Effective Date”), by and between Ahmed Musannif Rasheed (“Founder A”) and Leon Parsaud (“Founder B”), each a “Party” and collectively the “Parties,” with respect to Pixeloop LLC, a limited liability company organized under the laws of ______________ and operating publicly as Pixeloop Studios (the “Company”). 1. Purpose This Agreement establishes the ownership structure, capital and service contributions, vesting schedule, governance terms, and operational expectations of the Parties in connection with the Company. This Agreement is intended to operate alongside, and be incorporated by reference into, the Company’s Operating Agreement. To the extent of any conflict between this Agreement and the Operating Agreement, the Operating Agreement shall govern as to governance matters and this Agreement shall govern as to the economic and vesting terms set out herein. 2. Equity Allocation As of the Effective Date, the Company shall issue membership units in the Company (the “Units”) such that, on a fully-diluted basis immediately following issuance, the membership interests are held as follows: • Founder A (Ahmed Musannif Rasheed): forty-seven percent (47%). • Founder B (Leon Parsaud): forty-three percent (43%). • Founder C (Ibrahim Yoonus Rasheed): ten percent (10%). All Units issued to each Party (and to Founder C) shall be issued in full on the Effective Date and shall be subject in their entirety to the vesting and repurchase provisions set forth in Section 4. The Parties acknowledge that Founder C is the brother of Founder A. As a condition to the issuance and continued holding of Founder C’s Units, Founder C shall execute a Joinder to this Agreement on or before the Effective Date, acknowledging and agreeing to be bound by the vesting, intellectual property, confidentiality, non-compete, and dispute resolution provisions of this Agreement. 3. Contributions and Roles The Company is a new startup and studio that develops apps and games. The Parties acknowledge that tasks such as marketing, operations, and general business development are not exclusively siloed to one person, and all Parties are expected to collaborate and work across all necessary tasks to build the business. However, the primary focus areas and responsibilities for each Party are outlined below. 3.1 Founder A — Technical and Product Lead Founder A shall serve as the Technical Lead, Product Lead, and lead developer of the Company. Founder A’s primary contributions include, without limitation: • All software development, engineering, and technical architecture for the Company’s products and platforms. • Product management and roadmap definition. • Development environment, technical infrastructure, and code ownership prior to the Effective Date that is being contributed to the Company (described in Schedule B). • Day-to-day technical operations, code review, deployment, and technical hiring decisions. • Any capital contribution and pre-existing intellectual property identified in Schedule B. 3.2 Founder B — Marketing and Strategy Founder B shall serve in the following capacities, which may expand or change over time by mutual written agreement: • Marketing, brand growth, and audience development. • Business strategy, partnerships, and go-to-market execution. • Operational contribution and support of the Company’s growth. 3.3 Time Commitment Each Party shall devote such time and attention to the Company as is reasonably required to advance their respective responsibilities. Neither Party is required to commit to full-time employment with the Company, and each Party may continue to operate other ventures and engagements, provided such activities (a) do not directly compete with the Company’s then-current core business of developing and publishing apps and games under the Pixeloop brand and (b) do not materially impair the Party’s ability to perform their obligations under this Agreement. 4. Vesting and Repurchase 4.1 Vesting Schedule (All Founders) The Units issued to each of Founder A, Founder B, and Founder C shall vest over a total period of thirty-six (36) months from the Effective Date, with a one-year cliff. Specifically: • No Units shall vest prior to the first anniversary of the Effective Date (the “Cliff Date”). • On the Cliff Date, twelve thirty-sixths (12/36) of each Founder’s Units shall vest in a single tranche. • Thereafter, an additional one thirty-sixth (1/36) of each Founder’s Units shall vest on the same day of each month for the following twenty-four (24) months, until all Units are fully vested on the third anniversary of the Effective Date. Vesting is continuous and is not contingent on the subjective satisfaction of any other Party. Vested Units may not be unilaterally clawed back except as expressly provided in Section 4.3. 4.2 Acceleration In the event of (a) a Change of Control of the Company (including a sale of substantially all assets or a merger in which the Company is not the surviving entity), or (b) the termination of a Founder’s involvement by the Company without Cause, one hundred percent (100%) of such Founder’s then-unvested Units shall vest immediately (double-trigger acceleration upon Change of Control, single-trigger upon termination without Cause). 4.3 Repurchase Upon Departure If any Founder ceases to be actively involved in the Company, the following shall apply: • Unvested Units shall be forfeited and returned to the Company treasury at no cost. • Good Leaver: If a Founder departs (i) for any reason other than for Cause, (ii) due to death or permanent disability, or (iii) by mutual written agreement, such Founder shall retain all vested Units. The Company may, but is not required to, repurchase such vested Units at their then-current fair market value (“FMV”), as determined in good faith by the Parties or, failing agreement within thirty (30) days, by an independent third-party valuer selected jointly and paid for by the Company. • Bad Leaver: If a Founder is terminated for Cause (defined as gross negligence, willful misconduct, material breach of this Agreement that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice, fraud, or a felony conviction), the Company may, at its option, repurchase such Founder’s vested Units at the lesser of (x) original issuance price or (y) FMV. For the avoidance of doubt, voluntary departure for personal reasons, relocation, or to pursue another opportunity that does not compete with the Company shall not, by itself, constitute Cause. 5. Dilution and Future Issuances The Parties acknowledge that membership interests may be diluted in the future as a result of investor financings, employee or advisor equity grants, strategic partnerships, and the addition of new co-founders or executives. Such issuances are a normal incident of company growth and shall affect all Founders pro rata in accordance with their respective then-current ownership, unless otherwise agreed by all Founders in writing. 5.1 Pro-Rata Participation In any future issuance of Units or equity-linked securities by the Company for cash consideration (other than issuances under an approved option pool, conversions, or issuances in connection with a bona fide acquisition), each Founder shall have the right (but not the obligation) to purchase up to their pro-rata share of such issuance on the same terms, sufficient to maintain their then-current ownership percentage. 6. Information Rights So long as a Founder holds at least five percent (5%) of the Company’s outstanding membership interests, the Company shall provide such Founder with: • Quarterly unaudited financial statements (P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow) within forty-five (45) days after the end of each fiscal quarter. • An up-to-date capitalization table reflecting all issued and outstanding Units, option grants, convertible instruments, and reserved pools, updated upon any material change. • Reasonable access, upon at least five (5) business days’ written notice, to the Company’s books, records, and bank statements for purposes of verifying the foregoing. • Prompt notice of any financing transaction, material customer contract, or threatened or actual litigation involving the Company. 7. Decision Making and Governance 7.1 Day-to-Day Operations Each Party shall have authority over decisions within their respective functional areas (Founder A: technical, engineering, and product management; Founder B: marketing, strategy, and brand). Day-to-day operational decisions do not require the consent of the other Party. 7.2 Major Decisions Requiring Mutual Consent Notwithstanding Section 7.1, the following actions shall require the prior written consent of both Founder A and Founder B: • Any issuance of Units, options, warrants, or other equity-linked securities. • Any financing transaction, including the incurrence of indebtedness in excess of $10,000 USD. • Any sale of all or substantially all of the Company’s assets, merger, acquisition, or other Change of Control transaction. • Any voluntary dissolution, winding-up, or filing for bankruptcy. • Any amendment to the Company’s Certificate of Formation, Operating Agreement, or to this Agreement. • Adoption of, or any increase to, the Company’s equity incentive plan or option pool. • Any related-party transaction between the Company and any Founder, or any family member or affiliate of any Founder, involving consideration of more than $1,000 USD per transaction (or, in the case of Founder C or any family member or affiliate of any Founder, regardless of dollar amount). • Any hiring, firing, change in role, change in compensation, or change in equity of any Founder, or of any family member or affiliate of any Founder. • Any distribution or dividend to members. 8. Intellectual Property 8.1 Company IP All work product, intellectual property, branding, designs, software, code, concepts, business materials, and assets created by any Party specifically for, or in the course of providing services to, the Company on or after the Effective Date (collectively, “Company IP”) shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company. Each Party hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Company IP. 8.2 Prior and Independent IP Notwithstanding Section 8.1, the assignment in Section 8.1 shall not extend to any intellectual property, inventions, works, products, or business interests owned by, or developed by, either Party prior to the Effective Date, or developed independently of the Company on a Party’s own time without use of the Company’s resources or confidential information. Founder B’s prior and independent intellectual property and ventures are identified in Schedule A. Founder A’s prior and contributed intellectual property is identified in Schedule B. Neither Schedule limits a Party’s right to add subsequently developed independent works to their respective Excluded IP by written notice to the other Party. 8.3 License Back To the extent any of a Party’s Excluded IP is, with that Party’s prior written consent, incorporated into Company IP, that Party grants the Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license to use such Excluded IP solely as incorporated. No assignment of such Excluded IP is implied. 9. Confidentiality Each Party agrees to maintain in confidence all non-public information regarding the Company’s operations, finances, technology, codebase, product roadmap, marketing strategies, customers, and any other proprietary information (“Confidential Information”). Each Party shall use Confidential Information solely for the benefit of the Company. The obligations of this Section 9 shall survive the termination of either Party’s involvement with the Company for a period of five (5) years, except with respect to trade secrets, for which the obligation shall continue for so long as the information remains a trade secret. 10. Non-Compete and Non-Solicit During the period of active involvement with the Company and for twelve (12) months thereafter, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly: (a) engage in any business that directly competes with the then-current core business of the Company, defined as the development and publishing of apps and games under the Pixeloop brand; or (b) solicit any employee, contractor, or customer of the Company to terminate or alter their relationship with the Company. For clarity, this Section 10 does not restrict Founder B from continuing to operate or develop the ventures, apps, and business interests identified in or contemplated by Schedule A, nor either Party from engaging in passive investment activities. 11. Failure to Contribute If a Party materially fails to perform their obligations under this Agreement, the other Party may deliver written notice specifying the alleged failure. The receiving Party shall have thirty (30) days to cure such failure. If the failure is not cured within the cure period and the failure constitutes Cause as defined in Section 4.3, the Company may treat the failing Party as a Bad Leaver under Section 4.3, subject to the failing Party’s right to dispute such determination under Section 13. Nothing in this Section overrides the vesting protections of Section 4. 12. Company Failure or Dissolution If the Company ceases operations, dissolves, or is wound up, all vested Units held by any Founder shall remain valid and shall be treated in accordance with the Company’s Operating Agreement and applicable law in any liquidation. Unvested Units shall be cancelled and returned to the Company treasury upon the effective date of dissolution. 13. Dispute Resolution Any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall first be addressed by good-faith negotiation between the Parties for a period of at least thirty (30) days. If unresolved, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by a mutually agreed arbitration body, seated in ______________, and conducted in English. The arbitrator’s award shall be final and enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek injunctive or equitable relief in court to protect Confidential Information or intellectual property. 14. Governing Law This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of ______________, without regard to its conflicts of laws provisions. 15. General Provisions 15.1 Entire Agreement This Agreement, together with the Schedules attached hereto, the Joinder executed by Founder C, and the Company’s Operating Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement of the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous agreements, whether written or oral, including without limitation any prior founders or equity agreements between the Parties relating to the Company. 15.2 Amendment This Agreement may be amended only by a writing signed by both Parties. 15.3 Severability If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be reformed to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable. 15.4 Counterparts and Electronic Signatures This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures and signatures delivered by PDF or e-signature platform shall have the same legal effect as original signatures. 15.5 Notices All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email to the addresses the Parties designate from time to time, with confirmation of receipt.
